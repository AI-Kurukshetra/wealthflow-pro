## Business Strategy

### Monetization Strategies
* SaaS subscription tiers based on the number of advisors and clients.
* Transaction-based fees for trade execution and portfolio rebalancing.
* Premium features (advanced analytics and AI recommendations) as add-ons.
* White-label licensing to banks and financial institutions.
* Marketplace commissions for third-party financial products.
* Professional services for implementation, training, and customization.
* Data insights and market research reports as premium offerings.
* API access fees for third-party developers and integrations.

### MVP Scope
Core CRM functionality with client management, basic portfolio tracking, document storage, task management, and compliance reporting. Include essential Indian market integrations like NSE/BSE data feeds, rupee-focused reporting, and SEBI compliance templates. Mobile-responsive web app with basic analytics dashboard.

### Competitive Landscape
Existing players like Practifi focus on traditional CRM features for wealth management. Opportunities exist for platforms that deeply understand the Indian market nuances, offer regional language support, integrate with Indian financial institutions, and provide AI-driven insights tailored to Indian investment patterns and regulatory requirements.

### Key Metrics to Track
* Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
* Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
* Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
* Churn Rate
* Assets Under Management (AUM) tracked
* Number of transactions processed
* User engagement metrics
* Time to onboard new clients
* Compliance audit success rate
* API usage and integration adoption
* Mobile app retention rates
* Support ticket resolution time

### Go-to-Market Notes
Target tier-2 and tier-3 Indian cities where traditional wealth management tools are underserved. Partner with regional banks and financial advisors. Offer competitive pricing compared to international solutions. Provide extensive onboarding support and local language customer service. Leverage the growing digital adoption in Indian financial services and emphasize compliance with local regulations.
